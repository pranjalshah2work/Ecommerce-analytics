RECOMMENDATION 1 — IMPLEMENT A HARD DISCOUNT CAP OF 20%
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Linked to Insight 4

Problem:
1,166 orders (12% of all transactions) were discounted
above 30%, generating an average margin of -91.5%.
These orders are not driving loyalty or volume — they
are simply destroying value.

Recommendation:
Introduce a company-wide discount ceiling of 20% across
all product categories. Any discount above this threshold
must require CFO-level approval with documented business
justification. Internal data shows that orders with
discounts between 11-20% still achieve a healthy 17.5%
margin — meaning the business can remain competitive
without breaching profitability.

Expected Impact:
If the 1,166 orders currently at 30%+ discount were
capped at 20%, and assuming even a conservative margin
recovery to 10%, this single policy change could recover
an estimated $50,000-$80,000 in annual profit.

RECOMMENDATION 2 — STRATEGIC REVIEW OF FURNITURE CATEGORY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Linked to Insights 2 and 3

Problem:
Furniture generates $742,000 in revenue but only $18,451
in profit — a 2.5% margin that barely justifies the
operational cost of carrying, storing, and shipping large
items. Tables alone lost $17,725. The category is being
cross-subsidised by Technology and Office Supplies.

Recommendation:
Conduct a three-part strategic review of Furniture:

  STEP 1 — Discontinue or renegotiate Tables and Bookcases.
  These two sub-categories are structurally loss-making.
  Either renegotiate supplier costs, raise prices by
  15-20%, or phase them out of the catalogue entirely.

  STEP 2 — Eliminate all discounts on Furniture products.
  Given the already razor-thin 2.5% category margin, any
  discount pushes Furniture orders into loss territory.
  Furniture should be a zero-discount category.

  STEP 3 — Redirect marketing investment toward Technology
  and Office Supplies, which deliver 17%+ margins and
  far better return on sales effort.

Expected Impact:
Eliminating Tables and Bookcases losses alone recovers
$21,197 in annual profit. Redirecting just 10% of
Furniture marketing budget to Technology could generate
significantly higher returns per dollar spent.

RECOMMENDATION 3 — PRIORITISE HOME OFFICE SEGMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Linked to Insight 6

Problem:
Home Office is the smallest segment by revenue but the
most efficient, delivering a 14.0% margin versus 11.5%
for Consumer. The Consumer segment — despite generating
the most revenue — is the least profitable, suggesting
it is being over-discounted or sold a lower-margin
product mix.

Recommendation:
  STEP 1 — Increase targeted marketing investment in the
  Home Office segment. These customers buy at higher
  margins and represent an underleveraged growth
  opportunity.

  STEP 2 — Audit discount approvals for Consumer segment
  orders. The margin gap between Consumer (11.5%) and
  Home Office (14.0%) is likely driven by more aggressive
  discounting to Consumer customers. Tighten discount
  controls specifically for this segment.

  STEP 3 — Develop a Home Office loyalty programme to
  increase purchase frequency among this high-margin
  customer group. Retaining one high-margin Home Office
  customer is worth more than acquiring three low-margin
  Consumer customers.

Expected Impact:
A 5% shift in revenue mix from Consumer to Home Office
segment — without any other changes — would improve
overall margin by an estimated 0.3-0.5 percentage points,
worth approximately $7,000-$11,000 in additional profit.

RECOMMENDATION 4 — CENTRAL REGION INTERVENTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Linked to Insight 5

Problem:
The Central region generates $501,239 in revenue but
only a 7.9% profit margin — nearly half the West
region's 14.9%. This gap cannot be explained by volume
alone. It points to structural issues: either higher
discounting rates, an unfavourable product mix, or
elevated operational costs in this region.

Recommendation:
  STEP 1 — Conduct a Central region discount audit.
  Identify whether sales teams in Central are approving
  higher discounts to win deals and compare average
  discount rates across all four regions.

  STEP 2 — Analyse product mix in Central. If Central
  customers are buying disproportionately more Furniture
  and fewer Technology products, a targeted promotional
  push for high-margin categories could shift the mix.

  STEP 3 — Review shipping and logistics costs for
  Central region orders. Higher fulfilment costs in
  certain geographies can silently compress margins
  without appearing in the discount data.

Expected Impact:
Closing half the margin gap between Central (7.9%) and
the company average (12.5%) would generate an additional
$23,000-$25,000 in profit from the Central region alone.

RECOMMENDATION 5 — DOUBLE DOWN ON TECHNOLOGY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Linked to Insights 2 and 7

Problem:
Technology is the highest revenue category at $836,154
and delivers a 17.4% margin — the best in the business.
Yet it receives no differential strategic attention
relative to Furniture, which underperforms dramatically.

Recommendation:
  STEP 1 — Increase Technology SKU depth. Expanding the
  range of Technology products gives high-margin items
  more shelf space and drives revenue without the
  operational burden of Furniture.

  STEP 2 — Bundle Technology products with Office
  Supplies — the second strongest margin category at
  17.0%. Cross-category bundles increase average order
  value while maintaining healthy margins.

  STEP 3 — Protect Technology margins by enforcing
  strict discount limits. Technology's strong margin
  gives room for occasional promotional pricing — but
  discounts above 15% should require explicit approval
  to preserve the category's profitability advantage.

Expected Impact:
A 10% increase in Technology revenue — achievable
through expanded range and bundling — would generate
approximately $14,500 in incremental profit at current
margins, with no change to cost structure required.
