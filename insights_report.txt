
BUSINESS INSIGHTS REPORT
E-commerce Profitability Analysis
Period: 2015-2018

INSIGHT 1 — HEADLINE PERFORMANCE
The business generated $2.30M in revenue across 2015-2018,
yielding $286,397 in total profit at an overall margin of
just 12.5%. While revenue is growing year-on-year, the thin
margin signals that discounting behaviour and cost structure
are significantly eroding value creation.

INSIGHT 2 — THE FURNITURE PROBLEM
Furniture is the most capital-intensive category, generating
$742,000 in revenue — yet delivering only $18,451 in profit,
a margin of just 2.5%. By contrast, Technology achieves a
17.4% margin and Office Supplies 17.0%. Furniture is
consuming disproportionate resources — storage, shipping,
handling — while contributing almost nothing to the bottom
line. This is a critical strategic misallocation.

INSIGHT 3 — TABLES AND BOOKCASES ARE DESTROYING VALUE
At the sub-category level, Tables alone destroyed $17,725 in
profit — making it the single most loss-making product line
in the entire business. Bookcases lost an additional $3,472.
Both sit within the already weak Furniture category. These
two sub-categories are not just underperforming — they are
actively subsidised by profitable product lines.

INSIGHT 4 — DISCOUNTING IS THE BIGGEST PROFITABILITY THREAT
This is the most critical finding in the analysis.
Orders with zero discount achieve an average profit margin
of 34.0%. As discounts increase, margins collapse:
  - 1-10%  discount : 15.6% margin
  - 11-20% discount : 17.5% margin
  - 21-30% discount : -11.5% margin  (loss-making)
  - 30%+   discount : -91.5% margin  (severe losses)
Most alarmingly, 1,166 orders — nearly 12% of all
transactions — were given discounts above 30%, pushing them
into deeply loss-making territory. The business is
essentially paying customers to take its products.

INSIGHT 5 — REGIONAL PERFORMANCE GAP
The West region leads profitability with a 14.9% margin and
$108,418 in profit, closely followed by the East at 13.5%.
The Central region is a significant underperformer —
generating $501,239 in revenue but only $39,706 in profit,
a margin of just 7.9%. This suggests either a pricing
problem, higher discounting, or unfavourable product mix
in the Central region.

INSIGHT 6 — HOME OFFICE IS THE MOST PROFITABLE SEGMENT
Despite being the smallest segment by revenue ($429,653),
Home Office customers deliver the highest profit margin at
14.0%, compared to Corporate at 13.0% and Consumer at 11.5%.
Consumer is the largest segment by revenue but the least
efficient in terms of margin — suggesting over-discounting
or a less premium product mix sold to this segment.

INSIGHT 7 — REVENUE AND PROFIT ARE DECOUPLED
The most dangerous signal in this dataset is the gap between
revenue and profit across categories and regions. High
revenue does not equal high profit. Furniture proves this —
$742K in revenue, 2.5% margin. This decoupling suggests the
business is optimising for top-line growth at the expense
of bottom-line health, a strategically unsustainable path.
